A short manual for evaluating the driver.s seat at tall person sight of view

Ari-Jukka Eskelinen, Vice President of The Tall & Small Association Finland, Industrial Designer MA

The seat (not the Spanish one)

Longitudinal adjustment: One should check if the seat can be adjusted so that the angles of both the knees and ankles are reasonably open. Too sharp angles cause not only discomfort, tiredness and possible long term damage, but they make the fast and accurate moving of the feet from one pedal to an other hard or even impossible.

Height adjustment: A must, because the fixed seats are assembled in an average medium person height and therefore without it the distance between the seat and the ceiling is too small.

Seat tilt adjustment offers better thigh support to longer legs and simultaneously reduces the need of longitudinal adjustment making reach distance to the steering wheel and dashboard shorter and leaves extra leg room for the rear passengers.

Combined height and tilt adjustment: Quite nice even when only the rear end is adjustable like in some VW:s. Separate adjustments as in some Volvos give even better range of adjustments.

Adjustment of the back rest: I think this is a standard equipment in all modern cars, some older American cars do have non-adjustable back rests. Stepless adjustment is better both because it gives more opportunities and it can be changed during driving without fear of collapsing into sleeping position. On should also check out that the back rest can be turned enough straight upward position so that the steering wheels is in comfortable reach.

Adjustable lumbar support: an important equipment to tall persons because the original supports of the seats are in wrong places. Although the lumbar support is usually positioned too low the possibility of adding or reducing it can help the situation. Some models have lumbar support that can be adjusted at two different heights.

The head rest

Fixed head rests are almost always too low. Closest to reasonable are in some Volvos.

The height adjustment: one should check if the rest leaves too far back to offer any support in case of an accident.

The tilt adjustment helps reducing the distance between the rest and back of the head.

Steering wheel

Height adjustment is the more common one, but the less useful. If the angle of the wheel changes much, it means that the more important top half of the wheel escapes further away from the driver when in upper position. Mainly it just helps getting in and out of the vehicle.

The longitudinal adjustment should be fixed by law as the only way to adjust the distance between the wheel and the pedals suitable for both ends of the height scale.

Combined adjustment for a tall person: the wheel should be taken out as much as possible, but the height adjustment can also be something else than the upper most one.

Driver should be able to place his both wrists at the same time to the top (12 o.clock) of the steering wheel while his shoulders are slightly pressed to the back rest. Be sure to see the speedometer well.
