Our Cause Has Been Noticed:

Seura magazine 1/2001 (one of the leading weekly family magazines in Finland). 3 ½ page interview of two club members: Minna Marjamäki (150 cm)

and Ari-Jukka Eskelinen (193 cm, club vice president) about advantages and disadvantages of not being just an average citizen.

Tekniikan Maailma magazine 4/2001. Car cabin size test II. A seven-page test reportage of a tallish family of four trying to fit into 16 cars:

4 larger medium sized sedans

Ford Mondeo

Opel Vectra

Toyota Avensis

VW Passat

Renault Megane Scenic

Seat Alhambra

Chrysler Grand Voyager

4 SUV:s

Suzuki Grand Vitara

Jeep Cherokee

Mitsubishi Pajero

Chevrolet Tahoe

The test dummies were: a family of four (mother 182 cm / 43 shoe, father 193 cm / 46 shoe, son 6 years 128 cm / 36 shoe, daughter 4 years 110 cm / 28 shoe) and two 16- year-old students (the girl 185 cm / 45 shoe, and the boy 193 cm / 51 shoe) to demonstrate the family.s problems for about 10 years. They also tested how a mother and two children (one sitting face forward on a safety pillow, and the other sitting face backwards in a safety seat) fit into the back seats of the cars. It.s against the safety regulations to have a person under 150 cm on a front passenger seat equipped with an air-bag. The test parents are both industrial designers, who had worked as trainees for the Valmet Automotive Uusikaupunki Factory (home of the Saab Cabriolets, .late. Opel Calibra and Porshe Boxter). This along with the expertise of the staff of the Tekniikan Maailma magazine gives this reportage certain credibility.

The first part of the test was made one year earlier. Then they tested 10 small and 10 Golf/Astra class cars. (TM 6/2000).

Opettaja magazine 4/2001. Teachers magazine in Finland made a story about a school desk test sitting session for different sized persons.

University of Kuopio: a pro gradu study of mobbing in school. An inquiry was sent to 200 members of our club. The study was made by Ms Maarit Hynninen.
