ABOUT THE ASSOCIATION.S ACTIVITIES IN 2000

TO PROMOTE EQUALITY IN FINLAND AND

OUR PARTNERS:

 

Department of Clothing Physiology at the Kuopio University

Project on renewal of dimension tables for the clothing industry:

2,500 Finnish women were measured. Our proposal to measure the size of the foot was also fulfilled. The association organised one measuring occasion and informed the members about other occasions taken place in different places. Results have not yet been announced.

 

Mr Gunnar Jansson, EPM, Vice-Chairman of the group dealing with

Minority article 14 of the Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European

Union and

Mr Osmo Soininvaara, Minister of Health and Social Services:

A letter was sent to the above persons about the minority group of tall and small people, discussing their inequality and rights as consumers. We proposed to establish a work force to map disadvantages, to design improvements and to study means for international co-operation.

 

Department of Occupational Safety, Ministry of Social Affairs and Health

5th European Personal Protective Equipment Seminar

On the Ministry.s request, we took part in an international seminar dealing with the following topics: A change made in the Personal Protective Equipment Directive, advantages and risks of electrical business in the market of personal protective equipment and market follow-up. Two Finnish industrial designers discussed some aspects to be taken into account in respect to small and tall people. The designers were invited to take part in the next seminar, too.

 

Cars tested by .Tekniikan Maailma. (World of Technology), issue 2

Head: Which car is capable of housing a tall family with two children and car sets?

Note! Are big shoes too large for pedals?

Test 1. Price group under 150,000 FIM, 20 smaller cars

Test 2. Price group 110,000 to 511,000 FIM, 16 roomy cars

The tests were carried out on the Association.s initiative. Our members acted as a test family. Due to the alarming results got in the first test carried out on more cost-effective cars, we contacted Mr Ari Vatanen.

 

Mr Ari Vatanen, EPM, about revision of Finnish motor taxation:

We sent Mr Vatanen a letter discussing disadvantages of Finnish motor taxation. According to us, tall people should have a change to buy safer cars which would be roomy enough. We also published some articles on the issue, which arose public interest.

 

Media interested in fair for small and tall people

Co-operation with school health service

Last autumn the association celebrated its 5-year anniversary. In connection with the celebration, sales fair and other events were offered to the members. The .Opettaja. (Teacher) journal wrote on the issue: Small and tall pupils tested regulated school furniture. Health service for school children prepares a project on the issue. We have regular contacts with a research group and the management of the school health service.
